About Me




Growing Up

From a young age I have always needed a creative outlet. In middle school I would spend hours sewing custom, wild slumber party pajamas. They were always embellished with rhinestones (think early 2000’s fashion.) In high school I shifted my focus to multi-pocketed custom patchwork fabric purses. I even started my own etsy shop and shipped a few purchases internationally! As I started to think about colleges I had two paths in front of me, art school or pre-law.





What is Practical?

I took every art class provided in high school. Simultaneously I methodically ticked off enough dual credits and summer college courses to graduate from high school with two years of college completed. This allowed me to flee Texas for New York City to pursue a very practical degree in politics, economics, and philosophy. (Okay, so the philosophy wasn’t all that practical.) However, once I graduated I knew I did not want to go to law school.





Merging into my True Self

I spent a few months post-grad nannying and baking. And then luck figured in. By chance I was nannying the son of a product designer. He connected me with my first post-degree professional job. I started my first job in the headquarters of a luxury lifestyle brand. I worked daily with the founder, art director and VP of the company. This was the first environment that allowed me to merge into my true self - calling out my analytical and creative instincts and skills...showing how they worked together to create a potential career. After I left my first job I started studying user experience design and frontend development. I have finally found a career path that embraces my analytical and creative duality.



On the weekends you will find me...

  • Baking cakes
  • Hanging out with my daughter
  • Hosting a dinner party for my neighbors
